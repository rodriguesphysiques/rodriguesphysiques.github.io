GYM MYTHS AND MISCONCEPTIONs

As coaches, we always hear crazy new trends and myths within the fitness industry. If you are new to working out and ultimately creating healthier lifestyle habits, these new trends and ‘tips’ can make things extremely confusing and potentially derail you from reaching your ultimate goal.

Here are our top gym misconceptions and myths

Myth 1: Lifting weights makes woman big and bulky

Starting with the most common misconception of them all! As all bodies are different, and not everyone enjoys weight and strength training- it is important to understand that weight training is not the reason, nor will it ever be the reason for a ‘bulk’ to happen. When we speak about a bulking phase, it simply refers to progressive overload training and eating in a surplus, which helps you gain more muscle. Weight training on its own will never grow your muscles to the extent of looking bulky without the planned meals behind it. Testosterone is also a vital hormone that is needed for the growth of ‘big and bulky’ muscles. Weight training is as conducive to weight loss as it is to the growth of muscles. Never fear this type of training, if you love it, continue with it!

Myth 2: Cardio is the only way you are able to burn fat or achieve your weight loss goals.

While cardio is an essential element for burning fat and losing weight, you should never limit yourself to this form of training, especially if you do not like it. As mentioned above, strength training helps preserve muscles and helps you increase your muscle mass. Put simply, by training your muscles more, the more calories you will burn all day long. Choose the type of training that you enjoy the most to reach your fitness goals- if cardio is not one of them- then there is a high probability that it will not be sustainable in reaching your goals.

Myth 3: You can spot reduce fat in specific areas with certain exercises.

Spot reducing fat is NOT possible. The ‘bra bulge’ or ‘love handle’ exercises that are being created are an ineffective attempt to spot reduce fat. For women, we tend to store excess fat in certain areas, often in our hips, thighs and lower stomachs. Most often, these are the areas that we tend to struggle to reduce the most and the last parts that start showing differences. In order to reduce these areas, the same principle will always apply- to lose weight a calorie deficit is needed. To achieve any fitness goal most and the most effective way to reduce those stubborn areas of stored excess fat is through a structured and planned workout and meal routine.

Myth 4: If you are not sore or stiff the next day, your workout was ineffective.

We all have made the mistake of measuring the effectiveness of our workouts by how sore we are the next day. Being stiff and sore is extremely common if you are a beginner or starting to train once again. Stiffness can also come from a different form or way of training than what you are used to or if you mix up your workout routine- comes the common saying of ‘I didn’t even know I had muscles in those areas.’ However, if you are sore after every workout for an extended period of time- you will need to include more rest and recovery days to allow your body to adjust and adapt to the routine and enable your body to grow stronger each time.

Yay Or Nay? >

With so many of these myths going around, many of us starting our journeys or wanting to learn more about fitness and creating a healthier lifestyle- it’s no wonder we can easily get confused and overwhelmed when it comes to training. But we’ve got your back sis! As time goes on, more information becomes available and new things get taught- we will make sure to keep you in the loop of any further myths going around to ensure your journey is fun and always on the right path that is completely unique to you!
