NUTRITIONAL MYTHS vs FACTS

Have you tried different diets and nothing seems to work? Do you start a diet and never finish it?Are you doing something wrong? Why won’t anything change? If these questions are ones you’ve noticed yourself ask before then this is definitely the right place for you to be. There’s a common misconception about food- that if we limit the consumption of it or if we label them as good versus bad it will help us in our journeys and in our diets. But have you noticed that if you do that you never fully satisfied? You struggle sticking to the diet? So what if we told you that all that doubt was a myth?

“I CANT LOOSE WEIGHT NO MATTER WHAT I TRY!”

We have summed up 5 common weight loss myths that are far from true and we want to explain to you why

Myth 1:

Fad diets work for permanent weight loss

Fact: A fad diet is a ‘trendy’ weight loss plan that promises dramatic results within a short period of time- a quick fix. Typically, these diets are not healthy and do not result in long-term weight loss nor sustainability. Eliminating certain food groups deprives the body of certain nutrients it needs to function. Without these essential nutrients the body looks for other sources to draw nutrients from which can cause complications to the basic functionality of the body as a whole. By eliminating food groups, you will notice intensified cravings which will lead to over indulgence once you re-introduce those food groups back into your meals.

Tip: A balanced diet supplies the nutrients your body needs to work effectively. Without balanced nutrition, your body is more prone to disease, infection, fatigue, and low performance. That means eating everything you enjoy in moderation!

Myth 2:

Eating starches are fattening and will make me gain weight

Fact: Many foods that are high in starch, like bread, rice, pasta, cereals, beans, fruits, and some vegetables are low in fat and calories. They become high in fat and calories when eaten in large portion sizes or when covered with high-fat toppings like butter, cream, fried oil or mayonnaise. There are two types of carbs – complex and simple. Complex carbs digest slowly and simple carbs on the other hand digest quickly, and include mostly sugars and processed grains.

Tip: DO NOT BE AFRAID TO EAT CARBS! Once again, having a balanced diet provides your body with all the nutrients it needs as well consuming everything within moderation which will lead to long-term results.

Myth 3:

“I can lose weight while eating whatever I want.”

Fact: To lose weight, you need to consume fewer calories than what you burn through daily activity and exercise. This is known as a calorie deficit. It is possible to eat any kind of food you want and lose weight. You need to limit the number of calories you eat every day and/or increase your daily physical activity. Portion control is KEY.

Tip: When trying to lose weight, you can still eat your favourite foods—as long as you pay attention to the total number of calories that you eat. In this case calorie tracking is important to avoid over eating. Try eating smaller amounts of food and choosing foods that are low in calories. Also, be mindful of your general health, yes, we all enjoy a good take out or sweet treat but do not let that be the only source of nutrition in your body.

Myth 4:

Skipping meals is a good way to lose weight quickly

Fact: Studies show that people who skip breakfast and eat fewer times during the day tend to struggle in reaching fitness goals as those people who eat a healthy breakfast and eat regularly throughout the day. This may be because people who skip meals tend to feel hungrier later on, and eat more than they normally would. It may also be that eating many small meals throughout the day helps people control their appetites.

Tip: Eat smaller meals throughout the day that include a variety of healthy and nutritious foods. Be consistent in your meal plan and do not under feed your body, it needs the nutrients to function correctly!

Myth 5:

Eating after 8 p.m. will cause weight gain.

Fact: It does not matter what time of day you eat. It is what and how much you eat and how much physical activity you do during the day that determines whether you gain, loose, or maintain your weight.

Tip: If you eat late in the evening, allow yourself at least 2 hours to digest the food before heading off to bed, as sleeping directly after eating can slow your metabolism down which could potentially cause you to experience some weight gain.

Bottom Line

There is no Do’s and Don’ts to neither nutrition nor Good v Bad foods. It’s as simple as eating wholesome and nutritional foods, being mindful of what you consume and always appreciating the food you eat.

Food is a celebration of living and should not be seen as a daunting exercise that limits our well being. You should be able to enjoy all foods, and that’s what we at RP hope to teach you!
