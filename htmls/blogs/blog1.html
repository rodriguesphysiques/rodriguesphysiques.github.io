Progress Tracking and scale weight?

numbers on a scale do not determine your worthiness.

Let's dive into things...

FITNESS MISCONCEPTIONS

1. You're focusing too intensely on the numbers shown on the scale

Firstly, you could potentially be shaping up and sculpting the shape you're wanting to create, but the numbers on the scale are making you think otherwise. An important aspect of fitness that you have to remember is that muscle weighs more than fat and you could potentially be losing centimeter's while increasing your weight and gaining muscle. There is also so many inconsistent elements to the number show on the scale. Water retention, increase sodium levels and the stage you are within your cycle causes daily fluctuation

2. Do not let the scale control you.

It's crazy to think that numbers control so much of our thoughts, emotions, moods and motivation. And it seems easy to say that now, but we had learnt this the hard way. There is so much new and improved research that has deemed scale weight to be a relatively inconsistent means of tracking progress. Knowledge is power in fitness, just as much as it in everything in life. Knowing this information, we have become detached from scale weight. How many times did you climb on the scale and you lost motivation that day to not train due to it not changing? The scale has no control of who you are, how hard you're working, how good you actually look and how amazing you feel.

SO if it's not important then what?

What methods do you

suggest we use now?

We are not saying that it's not important, but it is not the only factor you should consider. There is new and improved means of tracking your progress and ones that increase your motivation. Tracking progress via photos and measurements is so much more rewarding than stepping onto that scale. There has been so many times where we have completed programs, that when we measure our progress, we weigh 2-3kgs more, but look so much leaner, toned or muscular.

So now, what do you suggest we do? What if I want to track my weight?

If you are wanting to track your weight, we do not tell you not to do it. Track your weight over a certain amount of time and then average it out. However, DO NOT FOCUS ON THE SCALE! The performance trackers we suggest you use is monitoring how your energy has increased and how you perform in the gym, look at how you feel in clothes and take photos. There methods are so much more accurate because they do not include so many fluctuations and aspects that change on a daily basis.
