Tips to improve nutritional intake

Mind over matter

Food is vital to building muscle or losing weight. If you don’t fuel yourself correctly you can expect either weight gain, enormous weight loss, tiredness, lack of sufficient energy or a slower metabolism.

Many of us feel conflicted with food… ‘Diets’ will help me get to where I want to be, but the truth is, that is far from true. If you cannot sustain your eating habits, it unfortunately will not work. It can be a hard task but it all depends on how much you are willing to become disciplined in controlling your intake.

Here are a few tips we’ve prepared to help you plan for a healthy and happier nutritional structure!

TIP #1: Listen to Your Body

Ever heard of the connection between mind, body and spirit? Well that is exactly what is needed. Listening to your body and its reactions to foods is important. Do you bloat when you eat certain foods? Do certain foods leave you constipated or make your metabolism slower than usual? Do you notice when you are full? You should ask yourself these questions. You would be surprised how much we neglect listening to what our body needs and the various ways that these needs are shown to us. By being conscious of these reactions we are able to eliminate or minimize consumption of foods that have negative reactions to our body’s natural being.

TIP #2: Enjoy Your Food

How many times do you rush a meal or gulp it before you even realize what you are eating? We never take the time to truly appreciate food. It’s the driving force of our being. No living organism can survive without it. So, sit back, take each bite as if it was your last. Focus purely on the flavours, textures, smells and colours and appreciate the nature of it. By appreciating the food prepared you are slowing the eating process down and most likely assisting your metabolism in the processing the foods easier and quicker.

TIP #3: Plan Your Meals

Your body is like a mechanism. It enjoys routine and consistency- and that is the key to control. When planning your meals your body memorises routines and starts to function accordingly. Irregular eating habits can create confusion leaving you with low energy, being tired, irritable and less productive. Structuring your meals will aid time management and most likely control your calorie intake. By practicing meal planning your body will begin to automatically signal when it needs food and it will become second nature to consume at structured times without having to be conscious of it.

TIP #4: Stay Hydrated

You might have heard that hydration is KEY to any health journey, and that sentiment couldn’t be more important, or true!

Water has so many benefits to your body especially if you're trying to lose weight:

Curbs hunger & controls calories

Reduces bloating

Promotes healthy skin

Flushes your body of toxins

Makes you feel energised & fuels your muscles

Maintains sodium balance

Try to consume 2-3L of water daily to maintain hydration. If you one that struggles to drink water, break it down to structure your water intake, for example; introducing a glass of water as soon as you wake up before breakfast and/or a glass of water just before bed. Track your water consumption to ensure you have drank the correct amount during the day as well.

TIP #5: Product of Elimination

How do I adopt a healthier lifestyle and eating habits if I don’t know how or what I am doing wrong? The first step is acknowledging bad habits. You need to come to terms with things and being true to yourself. Being accountable to yourself will only further your results. How did we do it? We live by the moto “Little by little, a little becomes a lot”. Do not expect yourself to instantly eliminate certain foods from your diet and think it will last forever. That’s why we introduce the product of elimination and substitution. Slowly start substituting certain foods that are in totality more nutritious and less in calories, By slowly changing these we adopted healthier habits and eventually the “diet” stigma became a lifestyle. You end up enjoying these products over others and eventually you forget those habits even existed

TIP #6: Increase your Protein intake

Protein is often referred to as the king of nutrients! Whether it is plant-based or pure protein sources, it plays an extremely vital element to your body’s composition and function. Aim to add a source of protein to each meal and snack. It will help you feel full for longer, curb cravings and make you less likely to overeat. It aids muscle growth and recovery giving you that shape you want!

TIP #7: Portion control

PPortion size helps you eat in moderation and keeps you from overeating. Large portions trick you into eating more and consuming extra calories when you don’t necessarily need them. This can really set you back. A general tip you can start off with is by using smaller plates. Plating helps you tremendously with portion control. Measure and use accurate portion sizes with a food scale, if possible. That way, you know exactly how many calories you are eating and if you hitting your nutritional targets. You would be surprised at how much you think you eating vs how much you are ACTUALLY eating.

TIP #8: Do NOT beat yourself up!

This is the most important! Remember that there is no such thing as the perfect eating habit. It is okay to reward yourself with a dessert every once in a while. Always keep a positive attitude about eating well and developing healthy eating habits. Punishing yourself for having a slip-up or overindulging will only lower your motivation and confidence. Do not make the mistake of giving up after one bad day, or start again on a ‘Monday’, each day is a new day to try again… and again.
